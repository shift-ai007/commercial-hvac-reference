# Commercial HVAC Energy Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate HVAC energy efficiency in commercial buildings. Each item includes what to check, what "good" looks like, and estimated savings from fixing issues.

## Envelope and Insulation

- [ ] **Roof insulation**: R-19 minimum for Florida commercial. Check for gaps, compression, or water damage
- [ ] **Window glazing**: Single-pane glass on east/west walls = massive solar gain. Low-E or tinted glass reduces cooling load 25-40%
- [ ] **Door seals**: Check all exterior doors for daylight gaps. Automatic closers on high-traffic doors
- [ ] **Loading dock**: Dock seals in good condition? Open dock doors pull in unconditioned air at 1,000+ CFM

## Equipment Efficiency

- [ ] **Unit age and SEER/EER rating**: Units over 15 years or below 10 SEER should be evaluated for replacement
- [ ] **Compressor staging**: Single-stage compressors run at 100% or 0%. Two-stage or variable-speed reduces energy use by 15-30%
- [ ] **Economizer function**: For units with economizers — test the damper and controls. Stuck economizers are the #1 source of wasted energy in RTUs
- [ ] **VFD on supply/return fans**: Variable frequency drives save 15-25% on fan energy. Payback typically under 3 years

## Controls and Scheduling

- [ ] **Thermostat setpoints**: 72-74°F occupied, 80-85°F unoccupied. Every degree below 72°F adds 3-5% energy cost
- [ ] **Occupancy scheduling**: AC should ramp down 30-60 minutes before closing (building thermal mass holds temp)
- [ ] **Night setback**: Verify the system actually raises setpoint at night. Test by checking unit runtime on building management system
- [ ] **Holiday schedule**: Systems running on Christmas Day and other holidays wastes $50-200/day per RTU
- [ ] **Demand limiting**: During peak demand hours (2-6 PM), pre-cool the building earlier and raise setpoint 2°F during peak

## Ductwork

- [ ] **Duct leakage**: Commercial duct systems lose 15-30% of conditioned air through leaks. Seal with mastic, not duct tape
- [ ] **Insulation**: Supply ducts in unconditioned spaces (above ceiling, in attics) need R-6 minimum insulation
- [ ] **Balancing**: Measure airflow at each diffuser against design specs. Unbalanced systems overcool some zones while undercooling others
- [ ] **Damper operation**: Zone dampers should be functional and controlled. Stuck-open dampers waste energy in unoccupied zones

## Refrigeration

- [ ] **Refrigerant charge**: Overcharged or undercharged systems waste 10-20% energy. Check subcooling and superheat
- [ ] **Condenser coil cleanliness**: Dirty condenser coils raise head pressure and compressor energy use by 10-30%
- [ ] **Evaporator coil cleanliness**: Dirty evaporator coils reduce capacity and efficiency. Clean annually at minimum
- [ ] **Refrigerant type**: R-22 systems should be replaced (phased out, expensive to recharge). R-410A is standard

## Electrical

- [ ] **Power factor**: Low power factor (below 0.90) means you're paying for reactive power. Capacitor banks correct this
- [ ] **Motor efficiency**: Standard motors → premium efficiency motors saves 3-8% per motor. Focus on motors running 2,000+ hours/year
- [ ] **Lighting heat load**: Every watt of lighting adds ~3.4 BTU/hr of heat the AC must remove. LED retrofit = double savings (lighting + cooling)

## Quick Wins (Lowest Cost, Highest Impact)

| Action | Typical Cost | Annual Savings | Payback |
|--------|-------------|----------------|---------|
| Fix duct leaks | $500-2,000 | 15-20% of HVAC energy | 6-12 months |
| Clean condenser coils | $200-500 | 5-15% of HVAC energy | 1-3 months |
| Fix economizer | $200-800 | 10-25% of HVAC energy | 2-6 months |
| Optimize scheduling | $0 (labor only) | 10-30% of HVAC energy | Immediate |
| Seal building envelope | $1,000-5,000 | 5-10% of total energy | 1-2 years |
